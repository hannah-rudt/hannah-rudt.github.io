Background

Loneliness plagues young adults. A 2021 study conducted by The Cigna Group found that 79% of adults aged 18 to 24 reported feeling lonely. Times of transition, such as starting college or moving to a new location post-graduation, can be especially isolating. Photo Bucket aims to alleviate this by incentivizing young adults to explore "bucket list" experiences in their new environments. These activities are captured and shared with friends.

Spring 2022




Methods

  • User interviews with college freshmen and recent college graduates to identify key issues
  • Affinity diagram to analyze pain points and common experiences
  • Journey map encompasses user transition process, physically and emotionally
  • Competitor research to understand existing market of "once-a-day" apps (e.g., BeReal)
  • Wireframing
  • Prototyping




Insights

  • Users wanted a tool to direct them to new experiences. Many struggled put themselves out there and wanted incentive to try new things. Daily challenges provide direction and motivation.
  • Users relied on tangential relationships in new environments. Users have the choice to invite friends or go solo and simply share the challenge with their network.
  • Many users felt uncomfortable navigating new geographies, especially in cities. The map feature guides users to their new challenge locations and pins old locations.
